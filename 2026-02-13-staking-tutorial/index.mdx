This tutorial walks through how to stake Atto from the wallet.

Staking lets you delegate your balance to a voter while keeping your funds liquid. If your account meets the current rules, it may receive variable rewards while remaining free to spend.

## What Is Staking?

In Atto's wallet, staking means assigning your account's voting weight to a [voter](/explorer/voters). ORV voting confirms transactions; eligible accounts may separately receive variable rewards under the current distribution rules.

**Atto's staking keeps your funds fully liquid**—you can spend them anytime. Eligible rewards use a 30-day effective-balance window, so recent balances affect the calculation without locking your coins.

## Reward figures

The calculation uses two figures:

1. **Base programme APY**: <Metric name="distribution.staking.annual-percentage-yield" suffix="%" />
2. **GSI input**: <Metric name="distribution.growth-stability-index" suffix="" /> from the [Growth Stability Index](/docs/growth-stability-index)

**Effective programme APY: <Metric name="distribution.staking.annual-percentage-yield.effective" suffix="%" />** (base programme APY × GSI), before the selected voter's delegator share and any cap-related reduction. An eligible account's rate may therefore be lower.

**These programme figures are not a promised user return. Eligibility, rates, timing, voter sharing, and cap effects follow the current rules, and ATTO's market value may fall.**

## How to Start Staking

Getting started with staking takes just a few minutes:

### Step 1: Ensure You Have Enough Atto

Under current rules, you need at least **$10 USD worth** of Atto in your wallet to be eligible for staking rewards.

<IdealImage className="block mx-auto"  img={require('./wallet-balance.png')} alt="Atto wallet balance" />

### Step 2: Open the Wallet and Navigate to Staking

Log into your [Atto wallet](https://wallet.atto.cash) and look for the staking or delegation section.

<IdealImage className="block mx-auto"  img={require('./wallet-staking.png')} alt="Wallet staking interface" />

### Step 3: Choose a Voter

Browse the list of available voters and select one to delegate your balance to. Consider these factors when choosing:

- **Share Percentage**: How much of the rewards the voter shares with delegators
- **Total Delegation**: Voters with lower delegation may offer better reward rates due to the 180M Atto cap
- **Reliability**: Check when the voter last voted to ensure they're active

You can also browse active voters in our [voter explorer](/explorer/voters).

<IdealImage className="block mx-auto"  img={require('./voter-list.png')} alt="List of available voters" />

### Step 4: Delegate Your Balance

Select your chosen voter and confirm the delegation. Your balance remains fully accessible—you're not locking anything.

<IdealImage className="block mx-auto" img={require('./delegation-confirm.png')} alt="Delegation confirmation screen" />

### Step 5: Receive Eligible Rewards

That's it! Under current rules, eligible rewards are normally calculated and distributed once per day. Just make sure to:

- **Stay active**: Make at least one transaction every 30 days to remain eligible
- **Log in regularly**: Open your wallet to receive any pending transactions

## Understanding Your Rewards

### Effective Balance

Eligible rewards are based on your **effective balance**, which is:

- The average of your daily balances over the past 30 days
- Capped at your current balance

This system rewards consistent holders and prevents gaming by briefly inflating balances.

### Voter Cap

Under current rules, each voter has a cap of **180 million Atto** in total delegated balance. If a voter exceeds this cap, rewards are proportionally reduced for its delegators, so total delegation can affect the variable reward calculation.

## Want to Run Your Own Voter?

If you're technically inclined, you can run your own voter node and request registration for voter rewards. Operating a node is distinct from reward eligibility; see [how to host a staking voter](/blog/how-to-host-a-staking-voter) for the setup and registration steps.
